# FAQ: Cards, Discipline, and VAR

## Q1: What is a yellow card?
A yellow card is a caution given for unsporting behavior, dissent, persistent fouling, time-wasting, or entering/leaving the field without permission. Two yellows in one match result in a red card.

## Q2: What is a red card?
A red card means immediate ejection from the match. It is given for serious foul play, violent conduct, spitting, denying an obvious goal-scoring opportunity by handball, or using offensive language.

## Q3: What happens when a player is sent off?
The team plays with one fewer player for the rest of the match (they cannot substitute the sent-off player). The player also faces a suspension for subsequent matches.

## Q4: What is VAR?
VAR (Video Assistant Referee) is a technology that allows match officials to review decisions using video footage. It can be used for goals, penalty decisions, direct red cards, and cases of mistaken identity.

## Q5: When was VAR introduced?
VAR was first used in a FIFA competition during the 2018 World Cup in Russia. It has since been adopted by most top leagues including the Premier League, La Liga, Serie A, and Bundesliga.
