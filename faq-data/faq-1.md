# FAQ: Basic Rules of Soccer

## Q1: How many players are on a soccer team?
Each team has 11 players on the field, including one goalkeeper. Most competitions allow 3-5 substitutions per match depending on the tournament rules.

## Q2: How long is a soccer match?
A standard match consists of two 45-minute halves with a 15-minute halftime break. The referee may add stoppage time at the end of each half to account for delays.

## Q3: How is a winner determined?
The team with the most goals at the end of regulation time wins. If the match is tied in knockout competitions, extra time (two 15-minute halves) and penalty shootouts may be used.

## Q4: Can players use their hands?
Outfield players cannot deliberately handle the ball. Only the goalkeeper can use their hands, and only within their own penalty area. A handball by an outfield player results in a free kick or penalty.

## Q5: What happens when the ball goes out of play?
If the ball crosses the sideline, the opposing team gets a throw-in. If it crosses the goal line, it results in either a goal kick (for the defending team) or a corner kick (for the attacking team).
