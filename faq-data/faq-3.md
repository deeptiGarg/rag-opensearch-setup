# FAQ: FIFA World Cup

## Q1: How often is the World Cup held?
The FIFA World Cup is held every four years. The tournament was first held in 1930 in Uruguay and has been played consistently since then, except during World War II.

## Q2: How do teams qualify?
Teams qualify through regional confederation tournaments (UEFA, CONMEBOL, CONCACAF, CAF, AFC, OFC) held over the two years preceding the World Cup. The host country qualifies automatically.

## Q3: How many teams participate?
Currently 32 teams participate, but this expands to 48 teams starting with the 2026 World Cup in the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

## Q4: Which country has won the most World Cups?
Brazil holds the record with 5 World Cup titles (1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, 2002). Germany and Italy have each won 4 times, and Argentina has won 3 times.

## Q5: What is the format of the tournament?
The tournament begins with a group stage where teams are divided into groups of four. The top two from each group advance to the knockout stage, which includes the round of 16, quarter-finals, semi-finals, and the final.
