# FAQ: Player Positions and Formations

## Q1: What are the main position categories?
Soccer positions are divided into four categories: goalkeeper, defenders, midfielders, and forwards. Each category has specialized roles depending on the team's formation.

## Q2: What does a center-back do?
Center-backs are the core of the defense. They mark opposing strikers, clear the ball from dangerous areas, and organize the defensive line. Teams typically play with two or three center-backs.

## Q3: What is the role of a midfielder?
Midfielders link defense and attack. Defensive midfielders protect the back line, central midfielders control possession, and attacking midfielders create chances for forwards.

## Q4: What are common formations?
Popular formations include 4-3-3 (four defenders, three midfielders, three forwards), 4-4-2 (four defenders, four midfielders, two strikers), and 3-5-2 (three center-backs, five midfielders, two strikers).

## Q5: What is a false nine?
A false nine is a forward who drops deep into midfield rather than staying near the opponent's goal. This creates space for wingers to cut inside and confuses defenders who are unsure whether to follow them.
