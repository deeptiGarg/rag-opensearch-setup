# FAQ: Offside and Fouls

## Q1: What is the offside rule?
A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-to-last defender at the moment the ball is played to them. They must also be actively involved in play for the foul to be called.

## Q2: Can you be offside from a throw-in?
No. You cannot be offside from a throw-in, goal kick, or corner kick. The offside rule only applies when a teammate passes or plays the ball.

## Q3: What is a direct free kick?
A direct free kick is awarded for physical fouls like pushing, tripping, or handball. The player can shoot directly at goal from the spot of the foul without another player touching the ball.

## Q4: What is an indirect free kick?
An indirect free kick is awarded for non-physical offenses like offside, obstruction, or dangerous play. The ball must touch another player before a goal can be scored.

## Q5: What is a penalty kick?
A penalty kick is awarded when a foul that would result in a direct free kick occurs inside the penalty area. The kick is taken from the penalty spot, 12 yards from the goal, with only the goalkeeper defending.
